Chapter 6 – Paid Search Advertising (Google Ads)

Why Paid Search Is Powerful

  • Search queries reveal strong intent.
  • You only pay when someone clicks.
  • Even small budgets can work if keywords and ads are chosen well.

6.1 – How Paid Search Works

  • User searches → Google matches against advertisers’ keyword lists.
  • Ads enter an auction → winners appear in search results.

6.2 – Choosing Search Targets (Keywords)

  • Goal: show ads only to people who want what you offer.
  • Group 8–10 tightly related keywords in each ad group.
  • Match types:
    • Exact match – most precise traffic, lowest volume.
    • Phrase match – balanced default.
    • Broad match – high volume, risky and often wasteful.

6.3 – Writing Ad Copy

  • Components:
    • Final URL (landing page)
    • Path (display URL)
    • Headlines (up to 15, 30 characters)
    • Descriptions (up to 4, 90 characters)
  • Responsive search ads rotate combinations to find better CTR.
  • Good ad copy includes:
    • Keywords (for relevance and bolding).
    • Clear value proposition (“Free shipping”, “24/7 support”).
    • Optional call to action (“Shop now”, “Get a quote”).

6.4 – Buying Ad Space (Bids, Ad Rank, Quality Score)

  • Ad Rank = Bid × Quality Score.
  • Quality Score components:
    • Expected CTR
    • Ad relevance
    • Landing page experience
  • Google uses a second-price auction: you pay just above the next highest competitor.
  • Bidding:
    • CPC – cost per click.
    • CPA – cost per acquisition (Google converts to internal CPC).

6.5 – Analyzing Results

  • Monitor:
    • Impressions
    • CTR
    • Average CPC
    • Conversions and cost per conversion
  • Diagnose:
    • No conversions → landing page or targeting problem.

6.6 – Google Ads Account Structure

  • Keywords → trigger ads.
  • Ad Groups → sets of related keywords and ads.
  • Campaigns → group ad groups; define budget, locations, language, bidding strategy.
