Chapter 7 – Display Advertising (Banner Ads)

7.0 – Intro

  • Display ads (banners) appear on millions of websites.
  • Most are ignored, but huge volume means the remaining clicks still matter.

7.1 – Understanding Display Networks

  • Display networks connect publishers (sites with ad space) and advertisers.
  • They standardize ad sizes, content rules, and run auctions to decide which ads appear.
  • Bidding models:
    • CPC – cost per click
    • CPA – cost per acquisition
    • CPM – cost per thousand impressions (common for display)

7.2 – Targeting the Right Audience

  • Audience targeting – demographics, interests, in-market audiences.
  • Placement targeting – specific sites or topics.
  • Too-narrow combinations (audience + placement + device) can kill reach.
  • Be careful with mobile: accidental taps can produce low-quality traffic.

7.3 – Banner Ad Design

  • Effective banners do three things:
    1. Attract attention.
    2. Communicate a value proposition.
    3. Invite action (CTA).
  • Value proposition should be understood in <= 5 words.
  • CTA is critical because default behavior is to ignore the ad.
  • A/B test one element at a time and evaluate both CTR and conversion rate.

7.4 – Retargeting

  • Retargeting = showing ads to people who visited your site.
  • Most valuable display tactic:
    • Higher probability of conversion.
    • Works well with dynamic product ads.
  • Use frequency caps and avoid annoying past customers.

7.6 – Consistent Messaging

  • Always match value prop, CTA and design between ad and landing page.
  • “Never Start A Marketing Campaign Without A Dedicated Landing Page” (NSAMCWADLP).
