Chapter 13 – Digital Marketing Strategy Framework

13.1 – Strategy Framework

  • A full strategy answers:
    • What is the business objective?
    • Which marketing effort type has priority?
    • Which channels will we use?
    • How will we reallocate budget over time?

Three Effort Types

  • Demand Generation – create demand in new people.
  • Demand Harvesting – capture people already wanting your product.
  • Loyalty Building – get past customers to buy again.

13.2 – Applying the Framework (Examples)

Example 1: Custom Tees (E-commerce)

  • Priorities:
    1. Demand harvesting (top priority)
    2. Loyalty building
    3. Demand generation (later)
  • Channels:
    • Paid search – capture “custom t-shirt” searches.
    • Social & display ads – visually showcase product.
    • Email – loyalty and repeat purchases.
    • SEO – small early investment for long-term traffic.
    • Social posting – minimal early priority.

Example 2: B2B Analytics Software

  • Priorities:
    1. Lead harvesting
    2. Demand generation (educating leads)
    3. Loyalty building (mainly via support, with marketing support)
  • Channels:
    • LinkedIn & Facebook Ads – find business professionals.
    • Display ads – cheap awareness to supplement social.
    • Paid search – experimental (may or may not work).
    • SEO & content – thought leadership and organic discovery.
    • Email – main channel for nurturing leads.

13.3 – Channels and Tactics

  • Each channel has different strengths:
    • Email – loyalty, lead nurture.
    • Paid search – demand harvesting.
    • SEO – long-term harvesting.
    • Social ads – demand gen + some harvesting.
    • Social posting – brand and engagement.
    • Display – awareness, retargeting.

13.4 – Resource Allocation

  • Every channel has diminishing marginal returns.
  • Optimal allocation = the next \$100 spent in each channel produces similar marginal profit.
  • Reallocate budget from lower-to higher-marginal-profit channels as data comes in.
