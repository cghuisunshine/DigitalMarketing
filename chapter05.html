Chapter 5 – Off-Site SEO, Links & Content Marketing

5.0 – Introduction to Off-Site SEO

  • Off-site SEO is about improving rankings by earning backlinks from other websites.
  • Originally Google used PageRank; now they use many link-related quality metrics.

5.1 – Understanding Links

  • Each link has:
    • <a> tag
    • href URL
    • Visible anchor text
  • Types:
    • Internal links – within the same site.
    • Outbound links – from your site to others.
    • Backlinks – from other sites to your site (most important for SEO).
  • Nofollow attribute tells Google not to treat a link as an endorsement.

5.2 – Popularity Metrics

  • Total number of backlinks.
  • Number of linking root domains (unique websites).
  • Link neighborhood – are linking sites spammy or reputable?
  • Anchor text – relevant anchor text is powerful but over-optimized patterns look manipulative.
  • Editorial vs non-editorial links & link diversity.

5.3 – Link Building

Editorial Links (Best)

  • Earned because others genuinely want to link to you.
  • Strategies:
    • Publish truly useful guides, tools, data, or infographics.
    • Outreach to people who might find it valuable.

Manual Links (Medium)

  • You ask for the link or submit your site, but the other side exercises judgment.
  • Examples: business directories, chambers of commerce, high-quality guest posts.

Manufactured Links (Low Quality)

  • Completely self-created links (e.g., comment spam, article farms).
  • Google often ignores or penalizes them if overused.

5.4 – Content Marketing

  • Content marketing is not only about links; it’s about persuading users.
  • Good content marketing:
    • Is valuable and interesting by itself.
    • Subtly increases desire to buy.
    • Avoids being too salesy or pushy.
  • Traffic sources for content:
    • SEO (ranking for informational queries).
    • Social shares.
    • Paid promotion (display, social ads).
