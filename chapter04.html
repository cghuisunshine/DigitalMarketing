Chapter 4 – SEO Foundations (On-Site SEO)

SEO increases organic search traffic by improving relevance and quality in the eyes of search engines.

4.1 – Maximizing Relevance

  • Give each page one primary keyword.
  • Place it in:
    • Title tag
    • URL
    • H1 (main heading)
    • Body content (naturally)
    • Image alt text
    • Anchor text of internal links (if appropriate)
  • Use keyword mapping: map specific keywords to the most appropriate page.

4.2 – Choosing Keywords

  • Use four filters:
    • Relevance – does searcher intent match your offering?
    • Search volume – how many people search this term?
    • Competition – how hard is it to outrank current pages?
    • Current ranking – are you already on page 2 or 3?
  • Long-tail keywords:
    • Longer phrases with specific intent.
    • Lower search volume but easier to rank and higher conversion.

4.3 – Creating High-quality Webpages

Behavioral Signals

  • Short clicks/bounces = poor relevance or quality.
  • Goal: keep users engaged and satisfied with the result.

Meta Descriptions

  • Short summary shown under the title in search results.
  • Write unique, readable descriptions that encourage clicks.

Performance & Content Quality

  • Fast load speed – essential for both SEO and user experience.
  • Avoid duplicate content; use rel="canonical" when necessary.
  • Create fresh, in-depth, and well-written content.

4.4 – Other SEO Considerations (Technical)

  • Fix orphan pages (no internal links pointing to them).
  • Use a logical internal linking structure; link more to important pages.
  • Maintain an up-to-date XML sitemap.
  • Ensure site is mobile-friendly.
  • Use HTTPS across the whole site.

4.5 – Local Search Rankings

  • Local businesses compete only against other local listings.
  • Key factors:
    • Google My Business/Profile claimed and optimized.
    • NAP consistency – Name, Address, Phone same everywhere.
    • Reviews – number and quality matter.

4.6 – Search Engines Past and Future

  • Algorithm updates (Panda, Penguin, Pigeon, Hummingbird, RankBrain) moved SEO from simple link counting to complex quality and intent understanding.
  • Knowledge Graph and voice search mean Google sometimes answers questions without sending traffic – long-term challenge for publishers.
