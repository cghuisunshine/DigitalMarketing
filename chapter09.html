Chapter 9 – Social Media Marketing Strategy

9.0 – Intro

  • For marketers, social media is:
    • A content platform with ad options.
    • A set of activities: posting, community management, customer service, listening.

9.1 – Social Media Activities

  • Customer acquisition
  • Loyalty and engagement
  • Brand building
  • Customer support
  • SEO support (content distribution)
  • Market research & listening

9.2 – 5-Step Social Media Plan

  1. Determine your objective (ads? support? brand? research?).
  2. Choose platforms (often start with Facebook; max ~3 at a time).
  3. Plan content – topics, formats, frequency, tone.
  4. Distribute & promote – mix of organic and paid.
  5. Measure success – with metrics aligned to your objective.

9.3 – Choosing Social Platforms

  • Match platform with:
    • Your audience demographics
    • Content type (visual, video, text)
    • Business goals (B2B vs B2C)
  • Don’t spread across too many platforms; mastery beats presence.

9.4 – Social Media Content

  • Content must satisfy:
    • Algorithm – engagement, watch time, shares.
    • Audience – relevance, value, entertainment, clarity.

9.5 – Distributing & Promoting Content

Paid Distribution

  • Simple: pay for impressions/clicks; platform ensures reach to target audience.

Organic Distribution

  • Harder; depends on:
    • Building followers
    • Encouraging shares/reposts
    • Algorithmic promotion of engaging content
  • Going viral is not a strategy; aim for consistent engagement.

9.6 – Measuring Success

  • Posting metrics:
    • Followers, impressions
    • Engagement (likes, comments, shares)
    • Clicks and conversions (if links included)
  • Ad metrics:
    • CTR, conversion rate
    • Be suspicious of inflated conversion numbers (view-through, etc.).
  • Listening metrics:
    • Volume of mentions
    • Sentiment
    • Topics
